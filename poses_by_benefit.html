Poses by Benefts
Enhance well-being with targeted poses.

These Poses are designed to calm the nervous system, reduce tension, and promote a sense of inner peace. It leads to various health and wellness benefits.

Strength & Toning

Plank Pose (Phalakasana)

A full-body strengthening pose that builds core stability, arm strength, and endurance. It engages nearly every muscle group.

Do's
  • Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
  • Engage your core and glutes.
  • Keep hands directly under shoulders.
Don'ts
  • Allow your hips to sag or pike up.
  • Lock your elbows.
  • Hold your breath.

Chair Pose (Utkatasana)

Strengthens the thighs, ankles, calves, and spine. It also stretches the shoulders and chest, building heat and endurance.

Do's
  • Sink hips down as if sitting in a chair.
  • Keep knees behind toes (or aligned).
  • Lift arms actively.
Don'ts
  • Arch your lower back excessively.
  • Let your knees splay out or fall inward.

Leg & Glute Strengthening Poses

Warrior II Pose (Virabhadrasana II)

Builds strength and endurance in the legs and core, while also opening the hips and chest. Improves focus and balance.

Do's
  • Align front knee over ankle.
  • Keep back leg strong and straight.
  • Extend arms actively from shoulders.
Don'ts
  • Lean forward over the front leg.
  • Allow front knee to collapse inward.

Goddess Pose (Utkata Konasana)

Strengthens the core, thighs, and glutes while opening the hips and chest. Builds heat and empowers the practitioner.

Do's
  • Keep knees tracking over ankles.
  • Sink hips deeply, engaging glutes.
  • Maintain an upright, strong spine.
Don'ts
  • Let knees fall inward.
  • Lean too far forward.
  • Lock your knees on the way up.

Calming & Grounding Poses

Happy Baby Pose (Ananda Balasana)

Gently stretches the hips, inner groins, and hamstrings while relieving lower back pain and calming the brain.

Do's
  • Hold the outer edges of your feet.
  • Keep your lower back pressed onto the mat.
  • Allow your hips to gently open.
Don'ts
  • Strain your neck.
  • Pull too hard on your feet.
  • Lift your lower back completely off the mat.

Reclined Bound Angle Pose (Supta Baddha Konasana)

A restorative pose that gently opens the hips and groin, stimulates abdominal organs, and helps relieve stress and mild depression.

Do's
  • Support your knees with props (blankets, blocks) if needed.
  • Allow gravity to gently open your hips.
  • Relax your whole body, especially your jaw.
Don'ts
  • Force your knees toward the floor.
  • Strain your inner thighs.
  • Hold tension in your jaw or shoulders.

Flexibility & Mobility

Standing Forward Fold (Uttanasana)

Stretches the hamstrings, calves, and hips while strengthening the thighs and knees. Calms the brain and helps relieve stress.

Do's
  • Keep a micro-bend in your knees.
  • Lengthen spine on inhale, fold deeper on exhale.
  • Allow head and neck to relax.
Don'ts
  • Round your back excessively.
  • Lock your knees.
  • Force the stretch.

Pigeon Pose (Eka Pada Rajakapotasana variation)

A deep hip opener that stretches the outer hips, groin, and psoas. Can be intense, but highly effective for hip mobility.

Do's
  • Keep hips relatively even.
  • Use props (blankets/blocks) under hips if needed.
  • Breathe into the stretch.
Don'ts
  • Feel pain in the knee of the bent leg.
  • Force your body into position.

Spine & Shoulder Flexibility Poses

Cat-Cow Pose (Marjaryasana-Bitilasana)

A gentle, flowing movement that warms up the spine and increases its flexibility. It also stretches the back torso and neck.

Do's
  • Coordinate movement with breath (inhale for Cow, exhale for Cat).
  • Move slowly and mindfully.
  • Keep wrists under shoulders, knees under hips.
Don'ts
  • Force the spine beyond its comfortable range.
  • Rush through the movement.

Thread the Needle Pose (Urdhva Mukha Pasasana)

A gentle twist and shoulder stretch that helps release tension in the upper back, shoulders, and neck, while also promoting spinal mobility.

Do's
  • Keep hips stacked over knees.
  • Rest on your shoulder and temple comfortably.
  • Breathe deeply into your upper back.
Don'ts
  • Force the stretch if you feel sharp pain.
  • Allow your hips to shift too far sideways.
